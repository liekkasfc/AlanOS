## Status
Draft

## One Sentence Description
Alan OS helps Alan discover information gaps, convert them into money-making hypotheses, execute one validation bet per day, and learn his personal opportunity pattern over time.

## English Mission
Help Alan build a lifelong ability to find asymmetric information gaps, validate monetizable opportunities with real people before building, and compound his personal revenue judgment through recorded execution.

## Chinese Mission
帮助 Alan 持续发现信息差，把信息差转化为可赚钱的假设，在做产品之前先用真实客户验证，并通过长期记录执行结果来沉淀自己的赚钱机会模式。

## Vision

### 1 Year
Alan OS becomes Alan's daily second-income cockpit.

Within one year, the system should help Alan:

- maintain a steady pipeline of InformationGaps,
- generate one Today's Bet on execution days,
- record validation outcomes without friction,
- distinguish weak curiosity from real revenue signals,
- identify the types of opportunities Alan is most likely to validate.

Success looks like a habit: Alan knows what to test today, why it matters, and what was learned.

### 3 Years
Alan OS becomes a personal opportunity intelligence system.

Within three years, the system should:

- understand Alan's MoneyDNA from historical outcomes,
- recognize repeating OpportunityGenome patterns,
- recommend better bets based on Alan's strengths and network,
- help Alan choose between service, content, consulting, productized service, and product paths,
- make second-income exploration less random and less emotionally expensive.

Success looks like compounding judgment: Alan can repeatedly identify opportunities that fit him before they become crowded.

### 10 Years
Alan OS becomes Alan's personal wealth-learning memory.

Within ten years, the system should:

- preserve Alan's long-term opportunity history,
- model how Alan's abilities, constraints, and market taste evolved,
- explain why past wins worked and why past misses failed,
- help Alan build income streams from recurring personal advantage,
- become a trusted personal operating layer for market discovery and execution.

Success looks like durable capability: Alan owns a repeatable way to see gaps, act early, and learn faster than his past self.

## Product Principles

### 1. Action Over Information
Information is useful only when it changes what Alan does. The system should compress discovery into action.

### 2. Revenue Signal Over Popularity
Popularity can be misleading. Revenue signals matter more than likes, shares, stars, or public hype.

### 3. One Today's Bet Per Day
Alan OS must choose. Multiple daily bets create avoidance through optionality. One bet creates execution pressure.

### 4. Service Before Product
A manual service can validate pain, language, budget, and buyer access before software exists.

### 5. Sell Before Build
The system should prefer outreach, offers, interviews, pilots, and pre-sales over product development.

### 6. No Customer, No Opportunity
If the system cannot name a reachable customer segment or buyer, it has not found an opportunity.

### 7. No Give-Up Rule, No Execution
Each bet should define when Alan stops, retries, pivots, or records the result. Without a give-up rule, execution becomes vague.

### 8. Rejection Is an Asset
Rejection improves the system when it is captured. A clear no can be more useful than silent enthusiasm.

## North Star Metric
Weekly validated revenue signal.

A weekly validated revenue signal is evidence from real people that an opportunity may produce income. Signals can include:

- paid pilot,
- deposit,
- signed letter of intent,
- serious budget-confirming conversation,
- booked sales call,
- direct request for a paid service,
- referral to a buyer,
- repeated rejection pattern that clarifies why the opportunity should be abandoned.

The metric is weekly because Alan OS should create a rhythm of execution without pretending every day must produce money.

## System Slogan
"Don't chase trends. Own the gap."

## Long-Term Direction
Alan OS should evolve in this order:

1. Foundation: define concepts, workflow, schemas, and operating rules.
2. Manual loop: help Alan run discovery, scoring, validation, and learning manually.
3. Memory loop: use historical results to improve scoring and selection.
4. Automation loop: automate repetitive collection and normalization only after the manual loop proves valuable.
5. Personal intelligence loop: recommend bets based on Alan's observed opportunity pattern.

The system should resist premature SaaS thinking. The first customer is Alan's future judgment.
